freeCodeCamp

freeCodeCamp is a nonprofit organization that organizes a series of free, interactive lessons, teaching website and web application development. Over the course of 400+ lessons, they cover topics like HTML, JavaScript, CSS, jQuery, Bootstrap, Sass, React.js, Node.js, Express.js, MongoDB, and Git.

Coffee-and-Code

Coffee-and-code sessions are informal get-togethers where you can hang out and code with other campers in your city. We host Coffee-and-Code at the University of Ghana.

During meetings, with first-timers, we cover topics like the history of the Internet, World Wide Web, browser, and introduce the fundamental languages of the browser, namely HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

For returning members, we workshop coding projects, either a project you're working on, one you suggest of your own, or any from a growing collection of tailor-made examples designed to showcase what the browser can achieve.
